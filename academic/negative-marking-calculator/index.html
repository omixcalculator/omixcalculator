What is Negative Marking?

Negative marking is a method used in almost all competitive exams to discourage random guessing. In this system, a certain percentage of your total marks is deducted based on the questions you have marked incorrectly.

Example

In a competitive exam, let’s say 2 marks are awarded for each right answer, and 1 mark is deducted for each wrong answer.

For 10 correct answers = +20 marks
For 5 wrong answers = −5 marks
Total Score = 15 marks

How to Calculate Negative Marking

Negative marking is calculated through a universal formula.

Formula
Total Score = (Total Correct × Marks per Question) − (Total Wrong × Penalty Value)

Formula Breakdown:
Total Correct: Questions you attempted correctly
Marks per Question: Marks awarded to each correct answer
Total Wrong: Questions you attempted incorrectly
Penalty Value: Marks deducted for each wrong answer
Total Score: Final score after negative marking

Understanding Various Types of Negative Marking

Negative marking varies from one exam to another. For example, NEET negative marking is different from UPSC. Similarly, SSC GD negative marking is different from SSC CGL.

1/3 Negative Marking Calculator

The 1/3 negative marking system is commonly used in UPSC exams. When you input 1/3 (0.33), the calculator uses the following logic:

  • Correct Answer: +2 marks
  • Wrong Answer: −0.66 marks
Scenario

Correct Answers: 80
Wrong Answers: 20

Calculation:

(80 × 2) − (20 × 0.66)
= 160 − 13.2
= 146.8 marks

1/4 Negative Marking Calculator

The 1/4 negative marking system is used in exams like SSC and Banking. If you calculate using 1/4 (0.25), our calculator uses this logic to calculate your score.

  • Correct Answer: +1 mark
  • Wrong Answer: −0.25 marks
Scenario

Correct Answers: 80
Wrong Answers: 20

Calculation:

(80 × 1) − (20 × 0.25)
= 80 − 5
= 75 marks

0.25 Negative Marking Calculator

In this marking system, 0.25 marks are deducted for each wrong answer when 1 mark is awarded for a correct response. This system is widely followed in SSC and Banking examinations.

The calculation method remains the same, using the standard negative marking formula.

How to Calculate NEET Marks with Negative Marking

The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) follows a fixed negative marking scheme.

  • Correct Answer: +4 marks
  • Wrong Answer: −1 mark
  • Unattempted Question: 0 marks
Formula
Total Score = (Total Correct × 4) − (Total Wrong × 1)
Example

A student attempts 150 questions out of 180.

Correct Answers: 120
Wrong Answers: 30

Step 1: Positive Marks -> 120 × 4 = 480
Step 2: Negative Marks -> 30 × 1 = 30
Step 3: Final Score -> 480 − 30 = 450 marks

UPSC Negative Marking Calculation

UPSC uses a 1/3 negative marking system. For every wrong answer, one-third of the marks assigned to the question are deducted. The final score is calculated using the same negative marking formula explained above.

FAQs

What does 1/4 negative marking mean?

It means the penalty mark for each wrong answer will be 0.25 (one-fourth) of the mark assigned to each right answer.

What does 1/3 negative marking mean?

It means that for each wrong answer, penalty marks will be 0.33 (one third) of the marks awarded to each right answer.

What is a 0.5 negative marking calculation?

In this method, half (1/2) of the mark assigned to each right answer is deducted as penalty marks.

How to Reduce Negative Marking

  • Focus on Accuracy, Not Attempts: Don't try to attempt all the question instead, rely on accuracy based on the mock test.
  • Use the Elimination Method: When you have a basic idea of the question but are not sure, try to eliminate at least 50% 0f the total options.
  • Avoid "Wild Guessing": Never mark a question based on gut feeling; if you don't have any idea, leave it.
  • Read the question twice: Look for miss keywords such as "NOT," "INCORRECT," or "EXCEPT" in the question.
  • Analyse Mock Tests: Before the exam, rigorously analyse your mocks and find strengths and weaknesses.
